Responsibilities As Loan Recovery Agent
and Head Of Engineering On Computer Preventive
Maintenance At Acorns Africa Investment

  1. Loan Recovery Agent

    • Contacting Debtors:
      The primary responsibility is to make outbound calls to debtors who have missed payments on their loans. These calls are made to remind them of their outstanding debt and to negotiate repayment terms.
    • Negotiating Repayment Plans:
      Engaging in discussions with debtors to negotiate repayment plans that are feasible for both parties. This may involve assessing the debtor's financial situation, understanding their constraints, and offering options for repayment, such as installment plans or settlements.
    • Providing Information:
      Clarifying any queries or concerns that debtors may have regarding their outstanding debt, including providing details on the amount owed, interest rates, payment schedules, and consequences of continued non-payment.
    • Documenting Interactions:
      Keeping detailed records of all communication with debtors, including call logs, notes on conversations, agreements reached, and any promises made by the debtor regarding repayment.
    • Adhering to Regulations:
      Ensuring compliance with legal and regulatory requirements governing debt collection practices, including guidelines outlined by government agencies such as the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB).
    • Maintaining Professionalism:
      Conducting interactions with debtors in a professional, respectful, and non-threatening manner. Avoiding harassment, intimidation, or abusive language during communication.
    • Escalating Unresolved Cases:
      If debtors are unresponsive or unwilling to cooperate, escalating the case to higher levels of management or legal departments for further action, which may include initiating legal proceedings or enlisting the services of collection agencies.
    • Providing Feedback:
      Providing feedback to management on the effectiveness of collection strategies, trends in debtor behavior, and any challenges or obstacles encountered during the recovery process.
    • Adhering to Company Policies:
      Following the policies, procedures, and guidelines established by the lending institution or collection agency regarding debt collection practices, customer interactions, and data privacy.
    • Achieving Targets:
      Meeting or exceeding targets set by management in terms of the number of calls made, the amount of debt recovered, and other performance metrics.

  2. Head Of Engineering

    • Developing Preventive Maintenance Plans:
      Creating and implementing preventive maintenance schedules and protocols for computer systems, including hardware, software, peripherals, and networking equipment.
    • Establishing Maintenance Procedures:
      Defining standardized procedures and best practices for conducting preventive maintenance tasks such as cleaning, inspection, calibration, and software updates to minimize downtime and prevent issues.
    • Asset Management:
      Maintaining an inventory of all computer equipment, including specifications, warranty information, and maintenance history, to track usage, identify potential issues, and plan for replacements or upgrades.
    • Monitoring Performance:
      Monitoring the performance and health of computer systems through diagnostic tools, performance metrics, and user feedback to proactively identify and address potential problems.
    • Scheduling Maintenance Activities:
      Coordinating and scheduling preventive maintenance activities to minimize disruption to operations and ensure that maintenance tasks are completed in a timely manner.
    • Training and Development:
      Providing training and guidance to maintenance staff on preventive maintenance procedures, troubleshooting techniques, and safety protocols to ensure effective and efficient maintenance practices.
    • Vendor Management:
      Collaborating with vendors and suppliers to procure spare parts, replacement components, and maintenance services, and managing vendor relationships to ensure timely delivery and quality of products and services.
    • Documentation and Reporting:
      Maintaining detailed records of preventive maintenance activities, including maintenance logs, inspection reports, and maintenance schedules, and generating reports to track maintenance performance and compliance with maintenance schedules.
    • Continuous Improvement:
      Identifying opportunities for process improvements and efficiency gains in preventive maintenance practices, tools, and procedures, and implementing measures to optimize preventive maintenance processes.
    • Compliance and Standards:
      Ensuring compliance with industry standards, regulations, and manufacturer recommendations for preventive maintenance practices, equipment specifications, and software updates to maintain system reliability and security.
    • Budget Management:
      Developing and managing budgets for preventive maintenance activities, including allocating resources for maintenance staff, equipment purchases, spare parts, and maintenance contracts.
